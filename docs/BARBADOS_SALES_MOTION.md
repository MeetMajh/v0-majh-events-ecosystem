# MAJH Events — Barbados Hotel Partnership Sales Motion

**Scope:** This is a MAJH Events tenant business document. It describes how
MAJH Events (the flagship tenant on MAJH OS) approaches revenue partnerships
with Barbados hotels as an event producer. It is NOT a MAJH OS platform
strategy document.

**Relationship to MAJH OS:** MAJH Events uses MAJH OS as its operating platform.
The hotel partnerships described here are MAJH Events revenue opportunities.
Hotels do not need to be MAJH OS tenants for MAJH Events to deliver value at
their venues. If hotels later choose to become MAJH OS tenants themselves, that
is a downstream MAJH OS sales opportunity, not a prerequisite for this motion.

**Status:** Informational. Represents current thinking about the MAJH Events
Barbados sales motion. Actual sales conversations, pricing, and pilot terms are
subject to founder discretion and hotel-by-hotel negotiation.

## 1. What MAJH Events is selling

MAJH Events is an event producer. It plans, produces, and delivers event series
(gaming tournaments, live music, corporate events, conferences, entertainment
programming) at partner venues.

To Barbados hotels, MAJH Events offers:

- **Event production expertise.** MAJH Events brings the event concept,
  marketing, audience, production crew, and equipment needed to run an event.
- **Venue partnership model.** MAJH Events does not compete with the hotel; it
  uses the hotel's ballrooms, F&B, housekeeping, and staff, and pays the hotel
  according to the partnership agreement.
- **Unified data and reporting.** Because MAJH Events runs its operations on
  MAJH OS, event data (attendance, F&B consumption, revenue, guest behavior,
  post-event satisfaction) is captured in one system and can be shared back
  to the hotel in unified reports the hotel could not produce on its own from
  their fragmented systems.
- **Recurring event series.** MAJH Events prefers ongoing programming (monthly
  gaming tournaments, weekly live music, seasonal conferences) that turns
  hotels' unpredictable event revenue into predictable income streams.

## 2. Why Barbados hotels specifically

Barbados hotels have real, identifiable pain points that MAJH Events is
positioned to address:

- **Off-season revenue gap.** Barbados tourism is highly seasonal. Hotels have
  significant unused capacity May through November. Event programming during
  low season smooths revenue.
- **Fragmented event operations.** Most hotels' event teams use spreadsheets,
  WhatsApp, and email to coordinate with event producers. Hotel PMS systems
  don't talk to producer systems. Post-event reporting is manual and
  inconsistent.
- **Limited data on event customers.** When a hotel hosts an outside producer's
  event, the guest data typically belongs to the producer, not the hotel. The
  hotel loses visibility into who attended, what they spent, and whether they
  would return.
- **Underserved by enterprise event software.** Cvent and Oracle Hospitality
  target enterprise-scale hotels and producers. Caribbean properties are often
  too small, budget-constrained, or complex for these tools.

MAJH Events has a specific advantage in Barbados:

- The founder is Barbadian with existing operational presence (Tradewinds RB,
  TRS at Barbados Airport, cultural fluency, relationship density)
- Small-island relationship networks — one strong reference generates warm
  introductions
- Existing operational history in the market (not a Silicon Valley outsider)

## 3. Market context

The following are general market indicators, not commitments or promises:

- **Event management software market:** approximately $19.9B globally in 2026,
  growing at high double-digit CAGR. This is context for market size, not a
  claim MAJH Events will capture material share of it.
- **Hotel management software market:** approximately $5.74B globally, dominated
  by property management systems (Oracle OPERA, Mews, Cloudbeds, Agilysys,
  SiteMinder).
- **The coordination gap between event producers and venues is genuine and
  under-addressed** by both categories of software. Event software helps
  producers plan; hotel software helps hotels run their property; neither
  handles the day-to-day coordination between the two parties.

The MAJH Events value proposition sits in that coordination gap. It is not a
claim to compete with Cvent or Oracle Hospitality directly. It is a claim that,
by being the party who both produces the event AND runs on a modern operating
system, MAJH Events can deliver hotel partners something their existing tools
cannot.

## 4. Proposed pilot structure

The following is a working framework for MAJH Events hotel pilots in Barbados.
Actual pilot terms are negotiated hotel by hotel.

### Phase 1: Proof of concept (approximately month 1-2)

- **Target:** One Barbados hotel where MAJH Events has existing relationship
  or warm introduction
- **Scope:** MAJH Events produces one event at the hotel using MAJH OS as its
  operational platform
- **What the hotel receives:** Unified event data (attendance, F&B consumption,
  revenue), post-event financial reporting, customer behavior summary,
  actionable insights they could not otherwise produce
- **What MAJH Events receives:** Operational experience, real event data
  flowing through MAJH OS, case study for further sales conversations, actual
  event revenue from ticket sales / sponsorship
- **Cost to hotel:** No platform fee (pilot). Standard venue rental and F&B
  arrangements per hotel's normal terms.
- **Time investment:** MAJH Events founder + operational team

### Phase 2: Paid partnership (approximately month 3-4)

- **Target:** Same hotel from Phase 1 (renewal), and potentially one additional
  hotel
- **Scope:** Ongoing event series (e.g., monthly gaming tournaments, quarterly
  corporate events) at partner hotel
- **Structure:** Hotel pays a partnership fee for the coordination platform
  access and per-event data delivery. Specific pricing to be established
  hotel-by-hotel based on scale and scope.
- **Deliverables:** Real-time event pipeline visibility, unified event
  financials, guest data sharing (per privacy and consent terms), quarterly
  performance reviews
- **Success criteria:** Hotel renews for the next quarter, or a second hotel
  signs

### Phase 3: Regional expansion (approximately month 5-6+)

- **Target:** Additional Barbados properties plus potential St. Lucia
  (leveraging Tradewinds St. Lucia Hub existing presence)
- **Scope:** Full MAJH Events service offering, adapter integration where
  hotel PMS systems support it
- **Success criteria:** Monthly recurring MAJH Events revenue from hotel
  partnerships covers a meaningful portion of MAJH Events operational costs

**Timeline notes:** These months are indicative, not committed. Actual timeline
depends on hotel receptivity, MAJH Events founder capacity, and how the MAJH OS
platform capability matures. If MAJH OS substrate work delays MAJH Events'
ability to onboard hotels smoothly, this timeline stretches.

## 5. The value proposition, said to hotels

For hotels and venues, the pitch is:

> "You already have a property management system. You already have a restaurant
> POS. You already have event inquiries coming to you by email, phone, and
> WhatsApp. What you don't have is:
>
> 1. A single place where your sales team, F&B team, housekeeping, and the
>    event producer all see the same event details
> 2. Automated reporting that shows exactly how much revenue events generate
>    for you versus rooms-only business
> 3. A system that remembers every event you've hosted — what worked, what
>    didn't, what the client spent — so you can sell smarter next time
> 4. Guest data from event attendees who weren't your hotel guests, so you
>    can market to them later
>
> We're not asking you to replace your existing tools. We're asking to partner
> with you on event production, and we bring a platform that connects to what
> you already have. You keep your PMS. You keep your POS. We add what they
> can't do."

For MAJH Events itself, the internal case is:

> "Every event we produce at a Barbados hotel is a proof point for the platform,
> a real-data input to MAJH OS's knowledge pools, and a revenue stream that
> extends our runway while we onboard external MAJH OS tenants. The hotel
> partnership motion also gives us the operational muscle we'll need if a
> hotel later chooses to become a MAJH OS tenant themselves."

## 6. What this is NOT

To prevent confusion for anyone reading this document without full context:

- **This is not a MAJH OS strategic pivot.** MAJH OS remains a vertical-
  agnostic multi-tenant platform. Hospitality is one of many verticals MAJH
  OS is designed to serve. The Barbados hotel partnership motion is a MAJH
  Events tenant activity, not a MAJH OS product strategy.
- **This is not a claim to build "the hospitality platform."** MAJH Events is
  a tenant that operates in the events and hospitality space. MAJH OS is not
  competing with Cvent, Oracle Hospitality, or any other enterprise event/
  hospitality software.
- **This is not a commitment to specific hotels or specific timelines.** Hotel
  partnerships are being explored, not signed. Timelines will move based on
  hotel receptivity and MAJH Events capacity.
- **This does not deprioritize construction.** Construction remains the target
  first external MAJH OS tenant per `docs/STRATEGIC_DIRECTION.md` §10. The
  MAJH Events hotel partnership motion runs in parallel and is orthogonal to
  the MAJH OS commercial priorities.
- **Hotels are not MAJH OS tenants in this motion.** Hotels are venue
  partners to MAJH Events. If a hotel later chooses to become a MAJH OS
  tenant, that is a downstream opportunity, not part of this motion.
